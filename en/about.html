VISION UP: Empowering the Future of English Education in Japan
VISION UP is dedicated to the success of after-school English conversation schools for children aged 3 to 12. We champion the importance of English education in Japan, providing a safe and nurturing learning environment. We prioritize a seamless experience for working parents, offering reliable after-school care. We foster a lifelong love of learning, cultivating critical thinking and communication skills. Through our proven model and dedicated team, we partner with those who share our passion for high-quality English education, creating a brighter future for children in Japan.

Proven Results and an Expanding Mission
Since the establishment of our first school in Denenchofu in 2016, we've been steadily expanding our reach, launching new schools annually across Tokyo, Kanagawa, Chiba, and Saitama. Leveraging over a decade of expertise gained through operating "Kids UP," we actively seek partners who aspire to establish new schools or enhance existing ones. We provide comprehensive support across various facets of education:

New Business Development:
Guiding entrepreneurs in establishing successful ventures within the education sector.

Curriculum Provision:
Offering our proprietary curriculum, developed on the foundation of applied linguistics.

Consulting Services:
Providing expert guidance and support to organizations focused on delivering exceptional education to children.

Staff Training & Development:
Equipping educators with the training and tools necessary to achieve and maintain excellence in daily operations, staff management, and lesson delivery.

Business Partnerships:
Collaborating with like-minded organizations to expand our successful business model into new regions.

Long-term Support:
Ensuring our partners have access to ongoing support and resources to adapt to the evolving needs of the education landscape.

We're committed to empowering our partners with the knowledge, resources, and support they need to make a lasting impact on the lives of children and contribute to the advancement of English education in Japan.
